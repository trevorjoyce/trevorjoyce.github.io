Where will Mecole Hardman end up this season for the Dawgs?

Webpage created by: Trevor Joyce

In the midst of spring practice, a former five star prospect is finally starting to make waves around town. Mecole Hardman, the former "do it all" athlete in high school spent his first year in a Georgia uniform learning the ropes on how to play defensive back. Considering he didn't play too much of it in high school, Hardman didn't see the field too much his freshman year besides some special teams.

However, whether it be a lack of recruiting, production, or injuries, it has become apparent that the wide reciever position is a concern to Head Coach Kirby Smart. So, with a year of college under his belt, Mecole, who always figured to eventually find his way on offense, is finally starting to take reps with the wide recievers.

Although spring practice has only gone on for a little over a week, Mecole has quickly become the talk of the town, making circus like catches in practice and looking like a natural fit on offense. He has even been seen running the wild cat package with Terry Godwin, taking handoffs at running back.

Whether or not we see Mecole taking snaps on offense at the annual G Day game on the 22nd, it should only be a matter of time before we see him on offense at some point this season.

As you can see from the chart above, the dawgs don't have much production returning as the lead wide out McKenzie left for the NFL. With an up and coming sophomore quarterback in Jacob Eason, it will be imperative this year to find him recievers who can be consistant and productive. Only time will tell if the Mecole Hardman experiment will work out on offense, but one can only hope that he can become the next two way star at UGA and follow in the footsteps of Georgia legends Hines Ward and Champ Bailey.
